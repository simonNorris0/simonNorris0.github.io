Synopsis

The main purpose of this page is to serve as a brief profile to go with my resume - to establish some of my basic characteristics and provide background information.

It is composed of four sections: a personal introduction, details about my education and my academic interests, my career aspirations, and the causes I support and the organizations that I am a part of.

My hope is that this page will be useful to anyone who is considering working with me, to paint a picture for them of the kind of person that I am.

The table of contents at the top of the page can be used to skip to any particular section of interest.

Personal Introduction

My name is Simon Norris. I am finishing the 2nd year of a bachelor of science at Athabasca University in the School of Computing and Information Systems (CiS), majoring in CiS with a minor in applied mathematics.

I am curious but risk-averse and I approach new challenges both boldly and carefully. I work thoroughly and independently, and I am always willing to learn new skills in order to find a better solution, or a better method for solving a problem.

I enjoy work when it involves solving challenging problems, building software and computing systems, as well as both logical validity and soundness. In my free time I also enjoy playing chess, building drones, launching model rockets, programming microcontrollers, tinkering with embedded computers in the garage, and writing about my experiences on my blog. I am passionate about computing, learning new things, and sharing my ideas and creations with others.

The biggest issue driving me forward right now is to gain work experience in the fields of Information Technology, Software Engineering, and Academic Research.

Thank you for taking the time to read this personal summary!

Education and Academic Interests

I intend to take senior level electives that reflect more development of the computer science core of my degree rather than the applications. This is also the intention behind a minor in applied mathematics, (although I do also just enjoy the logical and challenging problems that post-secondary mathematics has introduced me to).

So far I am most interested in the design of distributed computing systems, computer networks, data structures and databases, and computer security.

The senior level COMP electives I am pursuing this year most reflective of the kind of specialization I desire from my degree are:

  • COMP 466 Advanced Technologies for Web-Based Systems
  • COMP 489 Distributed Computing
  • COMP 418 Distributed Database Systems and Database Tuning
  • COMP 400 Computer and Network Security
  • COMP 410 Software Engineering
  • COMP 372 Design and Analysis of Algorithms

Career Goals

My primary career goal at the moment is to get a software engineering internship, or some information technology experience to assist with that, and to find a position as a research assistant.

Looking further into the future, I would like to continue to pursue advanced education and publish my own research. My dream jobs would be to become a software engineer specializing in the development of distributed operating system and database software, or to design and maintain large-scale networked computer systems. I also see myself eventually having a faculty position as a professor of computer science.
