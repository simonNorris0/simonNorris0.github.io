        <h3>Career</h3>
        <hr>
        <p>I am currently finishing the second year of a bachelor of science in 
        computer science. I am highly motivated to gain some information 
        technology work experience and to find a software engineering 
        internship this year.</p>
        <p>I plan to complete my degree by the end of this year (2026) or 
        early in 2027 by working on it through the spring and summer. In 
        order to maintain focus on this goal I am currently only looking 
        for part-time, and preferably remote, work.</p>
        <p>The senior electives I am taking reflect a specialization in 
        computer networks, databases, distributed computing, computer 
        security, numerical algorithms, and mathematical modelling.</p>
        <p>After completing my degree and gaining some work experience I 
        plan to go on to complete a Masters of Science in Computer Science or 
        to pursue a Masters of Engineering in Computer Engineering.</p>
        <p>Through my education and work I hope to become a skilled network or 
        software engineer. I enjoy system-level programming at the interface 
        between hardware and software the most, but also find the problems 
        posed by networked systems exciting. Distributed and networked 
        computing provides an environment where I can apply my education 
        towards the design and analysis of both software and architecture in 
        a highly useful skill.</p>
        <p>I decided to minor in applied mathematics for two reasons. Firstly, 
        because I have a passion for mathematics and very much enjoy the 
        study of it. Secondly, because I saw it as a way to take my degree from 
        "computing and information systems" to "computer science," and in that 
        regard I believe it reflects my desire to pursue computer science or 
        computer engineering further. I have also come to realize more and 
        more as I continue my degree how valuable understanding the mathematics 
        that underly digital systems is.</p>
        <p>The program that I am completing my degree through is an accredited 
        Canadian university <a href="https://www.athabascau.ca/index.html">
          (Athabasca University)</a>, but it is a fully remote degree. 
        I see this as both a challenge and a benefit towards my goals. 
        On one hand, I have felt the impacts of an independent education most 
        prominently in the lack of networking with fellow students and teachers 
        compared to a traditional university environment - I am working hard 
        with digital networking platforms, and by reaching out to fellow 
        distance learners in my program, to remedy this issue. On the other 
        hand, I recognize that my degree provides demonstratable proficiency 
        in the ability to be productive and to accomplish tasks while working 
        remotely. This proven independence is a value which a traditional 
        university does not showcase to such an extreme.</p>

        <section class="subsection">
          <h4>Drone Building and Programming</h4>
          <p>I am currently working on programming the flight controller and 
          radio frequency control module for a homemade quad-copter drone. As 
          for the actual drone build I intend to utilize public 3D-printers and 
          open-source STL files to create the plastic body and props. In doing 
          research on quad-copters in my free time I have learned a lot about 
          the advancements that made the modern quad-copter so cheap to produce 
          and readily avaialable. Perhaps the most interesting component is not 
          the main microcontroller but the modern motors that power the props. 
          These motors resemble the brushless DC motors I grew up tinkering with 
          but they have a critical improvement. The actuations of each of the 
          four individual motors that power the four props of the drones are 
          controlled by microcontrollers within the motors. These brushless, 
          electronically controlled motors have a high power-to-weight ratio as 
          a result - they were a huge advancement in the story of flight.</p>
